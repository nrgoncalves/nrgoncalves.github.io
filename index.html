About me

I am a Data Scientist at Unipart Digital. I focus on developing machine learning-powered solutions for the manufacturing and logistics sectors -- mostly for predictive maintenance, demand forecasting and resource allocation.

I work with a variety of modelling techniques including (but not limited to) deep learning, gaussian processes, bayesian active learning and probabilistic programming.

You can find (an always somewhat obsolete version of) my CV here.

Background

I did my Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge, where I used machine learning to study how the brain helps us see in 3D. Before that, I did a double M.Sc. at RWTH Aachen (Germany) and Trinity College Dublin (Ireland), during which I investigated how the brain responds to visual motion. You can track my publications here.
